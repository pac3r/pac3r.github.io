Gamified walking sessions, wherever your patient needs to walk

Stridelight is a wearable platform that projects interactive walking exercises onto the floor in front of the patient — turning a prescribed walking regimen into an engaging, goal-driven experience, and every session into objective gait data.

Interactive floors work. They just can't follow the patient.

Pacing lights on running tracks and interactive exercise floors share one insight: a cue to follow, instant feedback, and a visible goal keep people moving. But these systems are fixed in place — costly, confined to one room, unable to follow a patient down the corridor, let alone home. Stridelight carries the same principle on the body.

Worn, not installed

The patient wears a compact laser projector and simply walks. The exercise is projected onto the ground ahead — in a hospital corridor, a clinic, or a hallway at home.

Stabilized to the floor

Our stabilization system anchors the projection to the ground: the patient moves, the target stays put. It behaves exactly like an interactive floor — except it works anywhere.

Compatible with assisted walking

Because the patient walks at their own pace, Stridelight works with a walker, a cane, parallel bars, or a caregiver's arm. It adapts to the patient — not the other way around.
